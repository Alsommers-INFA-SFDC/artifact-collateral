Customer Type 1

B2B: Selling to Other Businesses

🏢

What It Is

Your customer is another company. You're selling products or services that help them run their business.

💼

Example

Salesforce selling CRM software to IBM. Enterprise deals with multiple stakeholders and formal procurement.

⏱️

Sales Cycles

Longer decision timelines with multiple touchpoints. Relationship-driven with high deal values.

🤝

Buying Process

Complex buying committees, formal RFPs, negotiated contracts, and multi-year commitments.

2
B2B Challenge

The Revenue Leak in B2B Sales

Today — Fragmented Accounts

Chaos Costs You Deals

Without identity resolution, the same enterprise account appears 15 different ways. Sales reps duplicate effort, fight over territories, and miss the complete picture of account value and engagement.

e.g., IBM shows up as "IBM", "International Business Machines", "IBM Corp", "IBM - IT Dept", creating confusion across your entire sales org
→
The Impact

Lost Revenue

30-40% of opportunities lost due to poor account visibility. Reps can't see total spend, miss upsell signals, and waste time on internal conflicts instead of selling.

Result: Longer sales cycles, lower win rates, frustrated sales teams, and millions in uncaptured revenue
3
B2B Solution

One Customer, One View, More Revenue

Before — Blind Spots

Flying Blind

Sales teams operate with incomplete account information. Can't coordinate across buying committee, miss cross-sell opportunities, and struggle with accurate forecasting.

e.g., Marketing engages procurement, Sales talks to IT, Customer Success owns finance — no one sees the full picture
→
After — Unified Mastery

Complete Account Control

Single golden record per account. See every touchpoint, identify the full buying committee, coordinate your team, and spot white space opportunities instantly.

Result: 23% increase in account penetration, faster deal cycles, happier sales teams, and predictable pipeline growth

Customer Type 2

B2C: Selling Directly to People

👤

What It Is

Your customer is an individual person buying for themselves or their household. Direct consumer relationships.

👟

Example

Nike selling shoes directly to consumers on Nike.com. Fast transactions, brand-driven purchases.

📊

Transaction Volume

High volume of smaller transactions. Quick purchase decisions driven by brand, convenience, and personalization.

❤️

Relationship Model

Brand loyalty matters. Experience-driven with multiple touchpoints across web, mobile, store, and social channels.

5
B2C Challenge

The Personalization Paradox

Today — Disconnected Channels

Generic = Ignored

Without identity resolution, the same customer appears as 5 different people across email, mobile app, website, and store. You can't personalize, can't calculate true lifetime value, and waste ad dollars.

e.g., Sarah buys in-store, browses on mobile, subscribes via email — you treat her like 3 strangers and blast her with irrelevant offers
→
The Impact

Lost Customers

60% of customers expect personalization but don't get it. Result: Low conversion, high churn, wasted marketing spend, and zero competitive advantage.

Result: Customers leave for brands that "get them" — you're left competing only on price
6
B2C Solution

Know Your Customer, Grow Your Customer

Before — Spray and Pray

Mass Marketing Misses

Generic campaigns yield low conversion. You retarget existing customers, send irrelevant offers, and can't measure what actually drives loyalty.

e.g., Promoting running shoes to someone who just bought running shoes — annoying them instead of delighting them
→
After — Personal at Scale

Precision Engagement

Unified profile across all channels. Personalized recommendations, accurate lifetime value, intelligent retargeting, and loyalty built through relevance.

Result: 15-20% lift in conversion, 25% reduction in churn, higher customer satisfaction, and measurable ROAS
7
Customer Type 3

B2B2C: Selling Through Partners to End Users

🔗

What It Is

You sell to a business partner who then sells to the end consumer. You care about both relationships.

📱

Example

Apple selling iPhones to Verizon, who sells to consumers. You need visibility into partner AND consumer behavior.

🎯

Data Complexity

Shared data needs, complex attribution, two customer types to track. Channel dynamics add layers of coordination challenges.

⚖️

Dual Relationships

Balance partner enablement with consumer experience. Need to support partners while building brand loyalty with end users.

8
B2B2C Challenge

The Broken Telephone Game

Today — Data Disconnect

Lost in Translation

Without identity resolution, you can't connect partner sales to end consumers. No visibility into consumer experience, partner conflicts over territories, and zero understanding of true brand reach.

e.g., You see Verizon's order volume but have no idea which consumers bought, what they think, or why they churn — partners guard that data jealously
→
The Impact

Millions Lost

Brand loyalty evaporates because you can't see or support the consumer. Partner incentives misalign. Warranty and support become chaos. You're flying blind on both sides.

Result: Partners frustrated, consumers abandoned, and you lose market share to competitors who control the full value chain
9
B2B2C Solution

Master Both Sides of the Equation

Before — Partner Black Box

Invisible End Customer

Partners own the consumer relationship. You can't track product journeys, optimize partner performance based on consumer outcomes, or provide direct support when needed.

e.g., Consumer calls you for support — you have no idea what they bought, where, or from which partner — poor experience for everyone
→
After — Full Visibility

Connected Value Chain

Link partner accounts to end consumers. Track product journey from warehouse to living room. Support consumers directly when needed while honoring the channel. Optimize partner incentives based on consumer satisfaction.

Result: Higher partner satisfaction, stronger consumer loyalty, predictable warranty management, and you win on both fronts
