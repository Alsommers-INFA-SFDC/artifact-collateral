How unified data models unlock analytics, operational efficiency, and business value

Technical Edition · 2026
Entity Type 1

B2B: Organization-to-Organization Data Model

🗂️

Data Structure

Hierarchical entity model. Organizations contain contacts, locations, and buying units. Relationships map to org charts and procurement chains.

🔑

Key Identifiers

DUNS number, Tax ID, domain names, parent/subsidiary relationships. Multi-level hierarchies with geographic and functional dimensions.

📊

Analytics Focus

Account-level aggregation, penetration metrics, wallet share analysis, buying committee mapping, and multi-touch attribution across long cycles.

🔗

Data Relationships

Complex M:N relationships. One contact maps to multiple accounts, accounts have parent/child hierarchies, and buying groups span organizations.

2
B2B Data Challenge

Fragmented Account Hierarchies

Unresolved — Duplicate Entities

Data Fragmentation

Same organization exists as 15+ records with inconsistent naming, hierarchy conflicts, and unmapped relationships. CRM, ERP, and marketing systems hold conflicting versions of truth.

e.g., "IBM", "International Business Machines Corp", "IBM - North America", "IBM Corporation" all treated as separate entities with different hierarchies
→
Business Impact

Analytics Failure

Inaccurate account-level metrics, broken hierarchical roll-ups, incomplete touchpoint attribution, and inability to calculate true account lifetime value or penetration.

Impact: Reporting errors, missed expansion signals, territory conflicts, and strategic decisions based on incomplete data
3
B2B Solution Architecture

Unified Account Graph

Before — Siloed Data

System Fragmentation

Each system maintains its own account master. No global identifier, no hierarchy governance, no cross-system reconciliation. Manual matching attempts fail at scale.

Pattern: CRM account "IBM-001" ≠ ERP customer "12345" ≠ Marketing account "org-ibm-na" — three disconnected records for one entity
→
After — MDM Implementation

Golden Record System

Single authoritative account entity per organization. Probabilistic and deterministic matching rules, survivorship logic, hierarchy governance, and propagated golden keys across all systems.

Value: Complete 360° view, accurate roll-ups, multi-touch attribution across all channels, predictive account scoring, and data-driven territory optimization

Entity Type 2

B2C: Individual-to-Individual Data Model

👥

Data Structure

Person-centric entity model. Individual profiles linked to households, devices, and interaction histories across digital and physical channels.

🔐

Key Identifiers

Email, phone, device IDs, cookie IDs, loyalty numbers, and hashed PII. Probabilistic matching required due to identifier variability and privacy constraints.

📈

Analytics Focus

Individual-level behavior tracking, customer lifetime value, segmentation, personalization models, churn prediction, and cross-channel attribution.

🌐

Data Scale

High-volume, high-velocity data. Millions of individual profiles with real-time interaction streams from web, mobile, store, and service touchpoints.

5
B2C Data Challenge

Disconnected Identity Graph

Unresolved — Multi-Profile Chaos

Identity Fragmentation

Single individual exists as 5+ profiles across channels. Email subscriber, mobile app user, in-store loyalty member, web visitor — all treated as separate entities with no unification layer.

e.g., sarah@email.com (web), +1-555-0123 (mobile), loyalty ID 87654 (store), device IDFA xyz123 (app) — four disconnected identities for one customer
→
Analytics Impact

Model Degradation

Inaccurate LTV calculation, failed personalization, broken attribution, wasted marketing spend on duplicate profiles, and inability to measure true cross-channel behavior.

Impact: Segmentation models trained on fragmented data, recommendation engines miss preferences, and marketing ROI calculations are 30-40% understated
6
B2C Solution Architecture

Unified Identity Graph

Before — Channel Silos

Unlinked Identifiers

Each channel system maintains its own customer ID namespace. No cross-channel stitching, no identity persistence across devices, no household-level aggregation.

Pattern: Web analytics tracks anonymous sessions, mobile app has user IDs, CRM has contact records, loyalty system has member IDs — no graph connecting them
→
After — Identity Resolution

Persistent Customer ID

Deterministic and probabilistic matching creates unified identity graph. Single persistent ID per individual, linked profiles across all channels, household clustering, and real-time identity updates.

Value: Accurate 360° customer profile, precise segmentation, effective personalization, cross-channel attribution, predictive LTV models, and optimized marketing spend

Entity Type 3

B2B2C: Hybrid Partner-Consumer Data Model

🔀

Data Structure

Dual-entity model linking organizations (partners) to individuals (end consumers). Requires both hierarchical account data and individual identity graphs.

🎯

Key Identifiers

Partner org IDs, consumer PII, product serial numbers, warranty registrations, and channel attribution codes. Junction tables link partners to consumers via transactions.

📉

Analytics Complexity

Multi-level attribution: partner effectiveness metrics, consumer behavior patterns, product journey tracking, and channel conflict detection across the partner ecosystem.

🔄

Data Flow

Bi-directional data sync between manufacturer, partner systems, and consumer touchpoints. POS data, warranty claims, support cases, and direct consumer interactions.

8
B2B2C Data Challenge

Disconnected Value Chain

Unresolved — Data Opacity

Missing Consumer Link

Partner transaction data contains consumer PII but no unified identity. Manufacturer sees partner orders but not end-consumer behavior, product usage, or satisfaction signals.

e.g., Partner system shows "iPhone sold to John Smith 555-0123" but manufacturer has no identity link to track John's device usage, service claims, or future purchase intent
→
Analytics Blind Spot

Broken Attribution

Cannot attribute consumer outcomes to partner performance. Warranty claims disconnected from sale channel. Support interactions lack purchase context. Brand loyalty invisible at consumer level.

Impact: Partner incentives misaligned with consumer satisfaction, warranty fraud undetected, support quality degraded, and brand loyalty unmeasured
9
B2B2C Solution Architecture

Unified Partner-Consumer Graph

Before — Disconnected Systems

Data Handoff Failure

Partner systems pass transactional data without identity keys. Manufacturer creates separate consumer records for direct interactions. No linking layer between partner-originated and direct-originated consumer data.

Pattern: Partner sale creates consumer record A, direct support interaction creates record B — same consumer, two unlinked entities across the value chain
→
After — Value Chain MDM

End-to-End Entity Graph

Unified entity model links partners, consumers, products, and transactions. Partner-to-consumer attribution, product journey tracking, warranty lifecycle management, and cross-channel support context.

Value: Partner performance measured by consumer outcomes, support quality improved via full context, warranty fraud detection, and brand loyalty tracked across all channels
